Workplace Wellbeing

A different approach to wellbeing

Wellbeing is often talked about, but rarely embedded.

Too often it's reduced to surface-level initiatives — perks, posters, or one-off gestures — without addressing how people actually think, decide, communicate, and cope under pressure.

At The Mind Department, wellbeing is understood as something deeper and more practical. It's about awareness, choice, and learning how to work with the mind in demanding situations — without needing to step away from ambition, responsibility, or performance.

This work is calm, clear, and grounded in lived experience.

Workplace wellbeing services

I work with organisations to deliver:

  • • Mindfulness talks and workshops
  • • Wellbeing sessions embedded into working life
  • • Practical tools for managing stress, focus, and pressure
  • • Support for sustainable performance and healthier workplace cultures

My approach complements existing wellbeing strategies and is designed to work within real organisational constraints — not as a tick-box exercise, but as meaningful support.

What sessions can include

  • • Mindfulness for focus, decision making and stress management
  • • Supporting sustainable performance (not burnout)
  • • Creating space for different working styles and voices
  • • A movement and meditation session tailored to your organisation

Sessions are practical, grounded and designed to integrate into working life — not sit alongside it as a tick box exercise.

One-to-one support for employees

Many organisations choose to fund one-to-one mindfulness support for individual employees as part of their wellbeing provision.

This provides personal, confidential support for staff members navigating stress, pressure or transition, delivered through an 8-week mindfulness course tailored to their working life.

This can be particularly valuable for:

  • • Senior leaders and managers dealing with high-pressure roles
  • • Employees returning from stress-related absence
  • • Staff going through significant change or restructuring
  • • Anyone seeking practical tools for managing workplace demands

Why this approach works

I bring over 15 years of experience in demanding professional environments, so I understand first-hand what it's like to function well on the outside while feeling stretched, reactive or quietly exhausted on the inside.

This work is not abstract theory. It's rooted in lived experience and designed for people who work in the real world — with real pressures, real deadlines, and real constraints.

Mindfulness is offered in a way that is calm, realistic, and human — not overly corporate, not overly spiritual, and not performance-driven.
